Ransomware

Lockers

The most common type of ransomware tactic involves restricting user access to files, folders, applications and even the operating system itself
through encryption, followed by a demand for a tranfer of money to the threat actor's bank account (usually in the form of cryptocurrency).

WannaCry is notriously known as one of the biggest ransomware attacks using this very same tactic.
Impacting over 300 organisations in over 150 countries and costing billions in damages, the origins were traced backed to the Lazarus Group.
